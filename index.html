College Assistant Professor of Economics

Girton College, University of Cambridge

Collin M. Constantine is a College Assistant Professor of Economics and Official Fellow at Girton College, University of Cambridge, where he also serves as Director of Studies in Economics.

Collin is an applied theorist working at the intersection of international macroeconomics, income inequality, and political economy. His research is motivated by real-world puzzles that standard models fail to explain, with a focus on foreign exchange stock-flow dynamics, banking and international finance, and macroeconomic policy in open economies. He also pursues a broader agenda on development, the distribution of income and wealth, and the political economy of conflict.

His recent work reinterprets the “Dutch Disease,” shifting the emphasis from resource windfalls to the role of central bank financing of fiscal deficits. He is also developing new perspectives on fiscal dominance, geoeconomics, and the measurement of macroeconomic fundamentals in balance-of-payments constrained economies.
