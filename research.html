Working Papers

Accounting for the Paradox of Plenty: Foreign Exchange Shortages amid BoP Surpluses
Examines how foreign exchange shortages can persist even in the presence of balance-of-payments surpluses, and explores the macroeconomic mechanisms that disconnect external accounting outcomes from actual foreign exchange availability.
Latest draft: October 2025
Overheating on Ice: Mis-measuring Inflation and Output Gaps in SOEs
Develops a framework for understanding how overheating can be mis-measured in small open developing economies when CPI inflation is a lagging indicator and foreign exchange market pressure is omitted from standard output-gap analysis.
Latest draft: August 2025
Tariffs, Fiscal Policy, and the U.S. Trade Deficit: A Macro-Finance Perspective
Reconsiders the relationship between tariffs, fiscal policy, and the U.S. trade deficit through a macro-finance lens, with attention to balance-sheet adjustment and external financing conditions.
Latest draft: June 2025
From Oil Rents to Money Creation: Fiscal Alchemy and the Path to Public Accountability
(with Tarron Khemraj)
Studies how oil-financed fiscal operations are transformed through monetary financing and quasi-fiscal mechanisms, with implications for transparency, accountability, and macroeconomic management.
Latest draft: December 2024
Political Pressure and Central Bank Monetization: Dutch Disease and Monetary Developments in Ghana and Guyana
(with Tarron Khemraj)
Investigates the role of political pressure and central bank monetization in shaping Dutch disease dynamics, with evidence from Ghana and Guyana.
Latest draft: July 2024
Throwing Darts and Missing the Bullseye: Fiscal and Debt Sustainability in Open Economies
Reassesses fiscal and debt sustainability in open economies by incorporating external balance-sheet constraints and macro-financial interactions.
Latest draft: April 2022 (new draft coming soon)
The Political Economy of Ethnic Income Inequality: Lessons from Guyana
Examines how ethnic inequality interacts with institutions, political conflict, and long-run development outcomes in Guyana.
Latest draft: April 2022 (new draft coming soon)
Growth–Distribution Trade-off in an Endogenous Growth Model: The Case of Dependent Economies
Develops an endogenous growth framework for dependent economies to study the trade-off between growth and distribution under structural and external constraints.
