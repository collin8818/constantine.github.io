Working Papers

Dutch Disease as a Money Shock: The Missing-Imports Problem

This paper develops a model in which the composition of fiscal financing determines whether resource booms generate Dutch disease effects. Resource-financed spending can be absorbed through imports, while money-financed spending raises domestic demand without expanding import capacity. The result is real exchange-rate pressure and contraction in the tradable sector. The paper shows that identifying Dutch disease empirically requires attention to monetary diagnostics.
Latest draft: May 2026

Accounting for the Paradox of Plenty: Foreign Exchange Shortages amid BoP Surpluses

Examines how foreign exchange shortages can persist even in the presence of balance-of-payments surpluses, and explores the macroeconomic mechanisms that disconnect external accounting outcomes from actual foreign exchange availability.

Overheating on Ice: Mis-measuring Inflation and Output Gaps in SOEs

Develops a framework for understanding how overheating can be mis-measured in small open developing economies when CPI inflation is a lagging indicator and foreign exchange market pressure is omitted from standard output-gap analysis.
Latest draft: August 2025
